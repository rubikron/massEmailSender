## Team Formation & Mentor Selection

**No teams exist at program launch.** The sequence is:

1. Applications open at the start of Fall Quarter
2. Program Directors review and vet applicants over the first ~4 weeks (screening for domain depth, problem clarity, commitment capacity)
3. Teams are formed from accepted applicants
4. Research Mentors review team profiles and **choose which team they want to supervise** — mentors are not assigned, they opt in to the team that fits their research interests
5. Program begins Week 4 with teams and mentors matched

This gives Research Mentors agency over who they work with, and ensures teams are vetted before any mentor time is committed.

## Roles

### Research Mentor (PhD / Masters students)
- **1 mentor per team**
- Advise one team through the full research process — methodology, experimental design, paper craft, venue selection
- Respond to team questions async; roughly **~1 hour/week**
- Mentor gets to **choose their team** from the vetted applicant pool
- **Co-authorship** on the submitted paper
- No logistics responsibility — that belongs to Team Leads and Program Directors
- Commitment: Fall Week 4 → Winter Week 8

### Team Lead (domain experts — from symposium contacts)
- **1 team lead per 2 teams**
- Domain expert and accountability layer — not a research expert
- Weekly check-ins, milestone tracking, communication bridge to Program Directors
- ~2 hours/week during active weeks
- Commitment: Fall Week 4 → Winter Week 8
- See: [[outreach/Team Lead Program Description]]

### Technical ML Advisor (industry practitioners)
- **2–3 advisors total**
- Deliver the ML Foundations Bootcamp (Fall Weeks 4–6)
- Implementation office hours during experimentation phase
- No ongoing weekly commitment after bootcamp

### Program Directors ([YOUR_FIRST_NAME] + [COLEAD_FIRST_NAME])
- Application review and admissions
- Cohort health, faculty coordination, operations
- Facilitate mentor-team matching

## Admission Criteria (Strict)

Applicants must demonstrate ALL of:
- Declared or demonstrated domain focus (coursework, research exposure, or substantive personal experience)
- A concrete, articulable problem space (not a fully formed RQ, but specific)
- Capacity to commit 5–8 hrs/week across two quarters
- Intellectual maturity — comfort with ambiguity and critical feedback

Prior ML/coding experience is welcomed but **does not substitute** for weak domain knowledge. A strong-domain / no-ML student beats a strong-ML / no-direction student every time.

### Application Process
1. Written application (domain background + problem space)
2. Program Director screening (filter for domain depth + problem clarity before mentor time is requested)
3. Interview with Team Lead and (where appropriate) Research Mentor
4. Research Mentor selects their team from the approved pool

## Deliverables Per Team

1. **Research Proposal** (1–2 pages) — due Fall Week 5, requires Research Mentor approval to advance
2. **Experimental Results & Analysis** — documented pipeline + metrics, due Fall Week 10
3. **Submission-Ready Research Paper** — target venues: IEEE Access, IEEE domain symposia, ACM conferences, interdisciplinary AI workshop tracks at NeurIPS / AAAI
4. **Research Showcase Presentation** — end-of-program symposium, Winter Week 8
