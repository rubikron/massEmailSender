# Symposium Outreach Email Template

## Usage
One email per recipient. Replace the bracketed fields, then send from AIC's official email.

## Template

**Subject:** `{Name}` — Help Lead a Research Team Through Publication (AI Collective)

---

Hi `{Name}`,

Congrats on your presentation, "{`{Presentation Title}`}" — `{1-sentence hook: reference something specific from their symposium abstract or research description. Example: "the idea of using sparse autoencoders to probe Chronos 2's generalization is really interesting" - also add a part that understands and respects the implications of their research}`.

I'm [YOUR_NAME], the Director of Research at the AI Collective Student RSO. We're launching the **AI+X Research Program** — a two-quarter research incubator that pairs participants' domain expertise with AI methods and guides teams to submission-ready papers at IEEE conferences.

I've been where you are — I published before, and one of the best parts was that rush of discovering something and having it validated by people in the field. It's an incredibly rewarding experience, and I want more students to get to feel that.

Because you've already done research in `{their domain/track}`, we'd like to invite you to be a **Research Team Lead**. You (along with other team leads) would supervise 2 teams (~2 hrs/week from Fall 2026 Quarter through Winter 2027 Quarter) and serve as their track/domain expert — helping with:

- **Ideation** — refining research questions that bridge their domain and AI
- **Statistical & analytical support** — experimental design, data analysis, methodology

You'd guide undergrad teams through the full publishing journey which is an incredible leadership experience. 

**Next step:** If you're interested, express your interest in this quick form — it takes 3 minutes:

[`{Google Form Link}`]

Responses close `{deadline date}`. We'll follow up with next steps within a week of your response.

**Want to see what AIC does before deciding?** We host regular events and fireside chats with industry leaders on topics like AI and job security, how AI is reshaping careers across industries, and more.

**[EVENT_DATE]** — [EVENT_LOCATION]
RSVP: [EVENT_RSVP_LINK]

If you have any questions, reply to this email or email me personally at [YOUR_EMAIL]

Best,

[YOUR_NAME] ([YOUR_EMAIL]) 
AIC Board — AI+X Research Program

---

## Placeholders (replace per recipient)

| Placeholder | Source | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `{Name}` | contacts CSV → `name` | First name only in greeting |
| `{Presentation Title}` | contacts CSV → `personalized_description` | Extract title from the presentation info |
| `{1-sentence hook}` | Manual or AI-generated | Reference their actual work — this is the personalization that makes it real |
| `{their domain/track}` | contacts CSV → `track` | e.g., tech / health / economy |
| `{Google Form Link}` | You fill this in | Same for all recipients |
| `{deadline date}` | You fill this in | Send date + 2 weeks |
| Event RSVP | Fixed | [EVENT_RSVP_LINK] — [EVENT_NAME], [EVENT_DATE], [EVENT_LOCATION] |

## Notes
- Total length target: ~180–220 words per email
- The `{1-sentence hook}` is the most important personalization — it proves this isn't a blast
- For recipients where the abstract is vague, use: "your work on `{topic}` caught our attention"
- For contacts at satellite/remote locations: no extra text needed, but don't reference in-person-only activities
