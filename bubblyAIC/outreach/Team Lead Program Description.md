# Team Lead Program Description

## AIC AI+X Research Program — Cohort 1

### What It Is
A two-quarter undergraduate research incubator that pairs students' domain knowledge with AI methods. Open to all majors. Each team goes from problem scoping to a submission-ready research paper, guided by research mentors (Masters/PhD) and technical ML practitioners. Teams target IEEE conferences and interdisciplinary AI venues for publication.

### Where You Fit
Team leads are domain experts and research mentors from within each track. You'll be paired with **2 teams each** (4–6 students total) and serve as their domain expert — guiding ideation, statistical analysis, and paper targeting, while keeping them on track and accountable.

### What You'll Do - working with the Program Director and Other team leads
- **Domain-guided ideation** — help teams refine research questions and scope problems that leverage AI in their domain
- **Statistical & analytical support** — advise teams on experimental design, data analysis, and methodology
- **Weekly team check-ins** (1 hour/week across 2 teams) — track milestones, flag blockers, keep momentum
- **Onboarding** — help new teams set up tools, clarify roles, understand deliverables
- **Milestone tracking** — ensure teams hits their milestones: research proposal (Week 5), experimentation (Week 10), and paper draft (Winter Week 5) deadlines
- **Communication bridge** — work on team issues with program directors; relay program updates to your teams
- **Peer review coordination** — organize internal paper reviews in Winter Quarter

### What's Expected
- **~2 hours/week** (check-ins + domain guidance) during active weeks (Fall Weeks 4–10, Winter Weeks 1–8)
- **~30 min/week** during lighter phases (bootcamp, showcase prep)
- Attendance at program kickoff and end-of-program research showcase
- You bring domain expertise from your own research — you don't need to teach ML, but you should be comfortable guiding teams within your area

### Program Timeline
- **Fall Quarter (Weeks 4–10):**
  - Weeks 4–6: ML Foundations Bootcamp (delivered by technical advisors)
  - Weeks 5–10: Problem scoping → research proposal → experimentation
  - Week 6 gate: Research mentor must approve each team's proposal to continue
- **Winter Quarter (Weeks 1–8):**
  - Weeks 1–3: Final experiments & analysis
  - Weeks 3–5: Paper drafting & internal peer review
  - Week 8: Submission to target venues + research showcase

### What You Get
- Leadership credential — you'll guide teams through the full publishing journey to IEEE conference submissions
- Deep exposure to interdisciplinary AI research across domains (tech, health, economy, policy)
- Research mentor connections across departments
- Opportunity to shape the institution's first AI+X research incubator
- A meaningful addition to your research profile: "Guided [N] teams to IEEE publishing journey"

### Qualifications
- Current student (any major, any year)
- Demonstrated domain expertise through research (lab experience, symposium presentation, publications, or substantive project work)
- Able to commit ~2 hours/week from Fall Week 4 through end of Winter Quarter
- Comfortable giving direct feedback, guiding ideation, and holding peers accountable
- Organized, proactive communicator
