## Email Template (Finalized 2026-05-24)

**Subject:** Research Mentor Opportunity — AIC AI+X Research Program

---

Hi `{First_Name}`,

`[HOOK]` — 1–2 sentences on their specific research or a named publication. Direct, no fluff.

I'm [YOUR_NAME], a board member at the **AI Student Collective (AIC)**. Having published with IEEE myself, I'm building a program that gives undergraduates a structured path to that experience — the **AI+X Research Program**, a two-quarter research incubator launching Fall 2026 (aisc.framer.website).

The program takes student teams from problem scoping through to a submission-ready paper at IEEE conferences and peer-reviewed AI venues. Applications open at the start of Fall Quarter; teams are formed from vetted applicants over the first four weeks. Research Mentors then review team profiles and **select the team they want to work with** — you have full discretion over who you advise.

The commitment is intentionally scoped: approximately one hour per week of technical guidance and fielding questions from the team, from Fall Week 4 through the Winter submission. The role is purely research — no logistics, no milestone management.

`[RESEARCH CONNECTION]` — 1 sentence on why their specific expertise is relevant to what teams in their track will be working on.

For mentors who make substantial intellectual contributions to the research, co-authorship on the submitted paper is something we're open to discussing.

If this is of interest, I'd be glad to send over the full program brief. Feel free to reply directly.

[YOUR_NAME]
AIC Board | AI+X Research Program
[YOUR_EMAIL] | aisc.framer.website

## Template Notes

- Co-authorship is floated as a possibility contingent on substantial intellectual contribution — not promised upfront. This is intentional and academically correct.
- "You have full discretion over who you advise" refers to the mentor-selects-team process (Fall Week 4, after applicant vetting). See master doc for full sequence.
- Tone: professional and direct. These are senior researchers — no casual language, no overselling.
- Length target: ~180–220 words per email
